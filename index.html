Clinical Trial Cost Estimation Calculator

Estimate full trial costs across start-up, staffing, participants, procedures, monitoring, data systems, intervention, safety, close-out, contingency and institutional overhead. Designed for protocol planning, grant preparation and budget negotiation.

1. Trial design and core assumptions

Edit the yellow fields. The calculator uses expected enrolled and screened participants to estimate operational burden.

Example: 60% means 500 screened to enrol 300.
Applies to monitoring, safety and QA intensity.

2. Start-up, regulatory and site activation

Include ethics, regulatory submissions, contracts, investigator meeting, site initiation and training.

3. Staffing and project management

Use monthly salary or cost-to-project values. FTE can be fractional.

4. Participant recruitment and screening

Screening cost is based on expected number screened, not just enrolled.

5. Visit schedule and procedure costs

Add each visit type. Per-participant cost = frequency × enrolled participants × expected completion. Dropout adjustment is applied to follow-up visits.

6. Monitoring, QA, safety, data systems and close-out

These items often become under-budgeted. Adjust according to trial risk, regulatory burden and number of sites.

7. Institutional overhead and reserve

Use this section to model university/institution overhead, inflation and a maintenance or contingency reserve.

This is an estimation tool, not a substitute for institutional finance review. Confirm salary scales, procurement rules, sponsor requirements, insurance, tax, indirect-cost policy and site payment arrangements before submission.
